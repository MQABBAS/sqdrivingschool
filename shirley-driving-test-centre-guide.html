Shirley Driving Test Centre — What to Expect

Shirley test centre serves learner drivers across Shirley (B90), Solihull (B91), Knowle (B93), Hall Green (B28) and surrounding postcodes. SQ Driving School instructors are experts on every test route that departs from Shirley — and they know exactly what Birmingham's DVSA examiners are looking for.

SQ Pass Rate at Shirley

Shirley test centre has a moderately high pass rate for well-prepared learners. The roundabout-heavy routes reward those who have practised lane selection consistently.

Typical Shirley Test Routes

Shirley routes typically involve the Stratford Road multi-lane corridor, Blossomfield Road with its speed camera, Haslucks Green Road residential sections, Bills Lane hidden junction, and Dickens Heath Road rural-to-urban transitions.

Inside Knowledge

SQ Driving School instructors have accompanied hundreds of learners through Shirley test centre. They know which routes are used most frequently and what examiners focus on. This local expertise is a major factor in our 90%+ pass rate.

Top 5 Challenges at Shirley

  • Stratford Road multi-lane — lane selection from 200m before junctions
  • Blossomfield Road speed camera — 30mph maximum, strictly enforced
  • Bills Lane junction — restricted visibility, approach slowly
  • Haslucks Green Road residential — pedestrian crossings appear frequently
  • Shirley roundabouts — appear on virtually every test route

SQ Instructor Tips for Shirley Test

  • Stratford Road: signal and lane-position 200m before every major junction
  • Blossomfield Road: 30mph from the start — camera is early in the road
  • Bills Lane: stop fully, look right then left, then right again before emerging
  • Haslucks Green Road: 30mph maximum — residents cross at random
  • Every roundabout: signal left on approach to 2nd and 3rd exits, right for last
