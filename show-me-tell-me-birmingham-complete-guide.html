How Show Me Tell Me Works in 2025

At the start of your driving test, the examiner will ask you one "show me" question (answered while driving, e.g. activate the rear windscreen demister) and one "tell me" question (answered before you set off, at the car). Each wrong answer counts as one minor fault. Two wrong answers = two minors. You cannot fail on show me tell me alone, but every minor matters.

"Show Me" Questions — Full List & Answers

These questions are asked while you're driving (at low speed or when safe to do so). Know them confidently — fumbling with controls during a question adds stress to your test.

Show me how you would check the engine has sufficient oil.
Pull out the dipstick, wipe clean, reinsert fully, pull out again. Oil level must be between MIN and MAX. Use the correct viscosity for your engine.
Show me how you would check the engine coolant level.
With a cold engine, check the level in the coolant reservoir — between MIN and MAX. Never open the cap on a hot engine.
Show me how you would check the brake fluid level.
Check the brake fluid reservoir — level must be between MIN and MAX lines. If low, check for brake wear or a leak.
Show me how you would check the power steering fluid.
Find the power steering reservoir (if applicable), check level is between MIN and MAX. Some modern cars have electric power steering — no fluid to check.
Show me how you'd check the windscreen washer fluid level.
Open the bonnet, find the washer reservoir (usually marked with a windscreen symbol), check level. Top up if needed.
Show me how you would check your tyres.
Check tread depth (minimum 1.6mm across central 3/4 of tread) and tyre pressure as per vehicle manual. Check for cuts, bulges or objects in the tyre.
Show me how you'd check the horn is working.
Press the horn — it should sound clearly. On test this is done while stationary.
Show me how you'd check that the headlights and tail lights are working.
Turn on ignition, switch on headlights, walk around vehicle to check both front and rear lights are illuminated.
Show me how you'd check the direction indicators are working.
Activate left and right indicators in turn and walk around the vehicle to confirm all four corners are flashing.
Show me how you'd clean the windscreen using the washers and wipers.
Operate the windscreen washer stalk to spray water, then activate the wipers. Ensure both are working correctly.

"Tell Me" Questions — Full List & Answers

These questions are answered at the car, before you set off. You'll be expected to physically demonstrate where relevant — not just describe.

Tell me how you'd know if there was a problem with your anti-lock braking system.
An ABS warning light would appear on the dashboard. If lit during driving, the ABS system should be checked.
Tell me how you'd check the tyres are correctly inflated.
Use a tyre pressure gauge when tyres are cold. Check against the tyre placard or vehicle manual. Adjust at a petrol station if needed.
Tell me what checks you'd carry out before using the brakes.
Check mirrors (rear and both door mirrors) before any braking situation. Ensure no vehicle is following too closely.
Tell me how you'd check the headlights and tail lights are working.
Explain that you'd switch on the lights and walk around the vehicle, or use a reflection in a window or wall to check.
Tell me how you'd check the direction indicators are working.
Switch on each indicator in turn and observe the flash rate on the dashboard. An overly fast flash indicates a failed bulb.
Tell me how you'd check the power-assisted steering is working.
With the engine running, gently move the steering wheel — a very stiff wheel indicates power steering failure. Check for warning lights.
Tell me where you'd find information on the correct tyre pressure.
In the vehicle handbook, inside the fuel cap, or on the tyre placard inside the driver's door.
Tell me how you'd know if your brakes were not working effectively.
The brake pedal would feel spongy, the vehicle would pull to one side under braking, or stopping distances would increase. Always act on brake warning lights immediately.
SQ Instructor Tip

Practise show me tell me under the bonnet, not just by reading. Open the bonnet on your instructor's car and physically point to each component — oil, coolant, brake fluid, washer fluid. Muscle memory beats cramming every time.

How many show me tell me questions are on the test? ▾

You will be asked one "show me" question and one "tell me" question — one of each per test. The questions are selected at random from the published DVSA list.

Can I fail my driving test for getting show me tell me wrong? ▾

Each wrong answer counts as one minor fault. You cannot fail on show me tell me alone, but since you're allowed a maximum of 15 minors before failing, every one counts. Most candidates pass with fewer than 5 minors total.

Do I need to memorise the answers word for word? ▾

No — the examiner wants to see understanding, not recitation. As long as your answer demonstrates you know what to check and how to check it, you'll receive a pass mark.
